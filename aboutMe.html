About the Journey

This has been the most fun, exciting, interesting, questionable, frustrating, rewarding, difficult, ambitious, worthwhile and gratifying venture that I have ever attempted.

The Places I Journeyed to

My very first stop was Coursera then I moved on to many other places to gain all the information I could. Some key places I visited are Free Code Camp,Codecademy, Plural Sight , BootStrap, JQuery, W3Schools, and Udemy to name a few. In addition, I have read several excerpts from books like Eloquent Javascript and Pro-Git.I dabbled a bit in Big Nerd Ranch and participated in some online coding Bootcamps. I have been better at version control and can find my way around a terminal( with a little help)! I also challenged myself with some projects I found online and ones I had some help with. This journey has not been on my own, My son has helped more than you can imagine and of course the countless number of places/forums on the web to get help. Thank You Stack Over Flow , MDN, Free Code Camp and the many other resources.

What I discovered on My Journey

Computer programming was initially very intimidating to me. But, I took a course on HTML and had a decent understanding in about a month. So onward to CSS and wow not so bad. There is always alot to learn but also lots of resources and a sense of accomplishment when you see that website take shape. Then came Javascript. I thought since HTML and CSS wasn't so bad, how hard can javascript be? I found very quickly why I was so intimidatd in the beginning. Javascript was not easy for me to grasp and I still struggle but I know that given the chance and some guidance I can make great things happen.

What the Next Journey is

The challenging road of seeking lucrative employment as a self taught web developer is where I am heading to now. I am hoping to work with a team of programmers that can help enhance my skills and understanding while building some really awesome sites together. Wish me luck!

The Temporary Destination
